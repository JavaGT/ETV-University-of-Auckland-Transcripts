pts/library_upload/3945_193037/Weekly_Review_No_340LIBRARY - Archive NZ - Weekly Review No 340(1948) News Clips: RNZAF Parade: Chief of Air Staff Air Vice Marshall Neville inspects a group of graduating ground crew personnel at Wigram Air Base and new recruits who've arrived to replace them in the training scheme. 0.1.20 Waitangi Ceremony: The 1948 ceremony at the Waitangi Treaty Grounds. 02.30 Tennis, Aust v NZ: In the Hutt Valley, visiting Australian tennis players compete against a team of 8 Kiwis. The visitors won 22 matches out of 23. Doubles and singles matches are shown. 05.30 Cape Kidnappers Gannets: Visitors arrive at the Cape Kidnappers Bird Sanctuary to view the large mainland gannet colony in the world. Nearly 5000 gannets live on the Cape and their population is increasing. Chicks are shown just out of the egg and bigger fluffy ones. Then they lose their fluff and the quills and wings begin to grow. The chicks reach deeply into their parents' throats to recover food that has already been swallowed and digested.
