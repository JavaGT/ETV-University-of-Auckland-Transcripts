pts/library_upload/3627_383778/Weekly_Review_No_140_Easter_Action_on_BourgainvilleLIBRARY - Archive NZ - Weekly Review No 140 - Easter Action on Bougainville(1944) While on the home front, most New Zealanders were taking it easy for four days over Easter, Americans, Fijians and some New Zealanders were fighting the Japanese in the shell-blasted swamps and jungle of Bougainville Island. Though the 3rd. N.Z. Division was already established 150 miles to the north on Nissan Island, the Japanese cut off on Bougainville were still fighting desperately. These grim pictures were taken under fire by a N.Z. National Film Unit Cameraman. Warships bombard the coast of a tropic island, the troops swarm over the beaches, and push inland against the Japanese. The camera crew joins a Fijian patrol and fight a fierce action at close quarters in the jungle until the main force comes up and blasts the enemy pillboxes with grenades and flame throwers.
