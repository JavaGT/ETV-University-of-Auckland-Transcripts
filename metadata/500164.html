pts/library_upload/3624_966611/Toe_Hold_on_a_HarbourLIBRARY - Archive NZ - Toe Hold on a Harbour(1966) A swinging, sometimes humorous treatment of the theme that the character of Wellington has been shaped by its peculiar location. Flanked on one side by steep hills and on the other by a deep harbour, the city has grown on a site more perpendicular than horizontal. People run 'obstacle races' down zigzags to reach the city centre. Great ships are berthed at the quays alongside. A bright fringe of beaches curves for 32 miles around.
