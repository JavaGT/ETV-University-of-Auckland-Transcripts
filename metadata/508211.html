Despite vows to pivot away from oil and gas, fossil fuel companies continue to make record profits, leaving those concerned with the environment to wonder if anything can force polluting corporations to reform their practices. Some see a path for change in climate litigation – including a new shareholder lawsuit against Shell that says its corporate directors aren’t transitioning fast enough to renewables. The case is the first of its kind to target a company’s board of directors in a climate lawsuit. In recent years, climate litigation has become a more frequent strategy in pressuring governments and corporations to confront their roles in the global climate emergency.
