pts/library_upload/4023_209564/Weekly_Review_No_120LIBRARY - Archive NZ - Weekly Review No 120(1943) Wartime Industry: Making Tennis Balls: Stocks of tennis balls in New Zealand have become exhausted because, although some rubber can be spared, workers cannot. So a group of Auckland tennis fans came forward and offered to make the balls in their spare time. This clip shows the production process. 02.05 Science in Industry, Testing Die Castings: At the Dominion Laboratory, munitions parts are tested in a steam oven that causes enough deterioration in the metal as would normally take place in a year. To find out what's wrong with an inferior alloy, samples are boiled in acid. Other samples of die casted metal are tested in a spectrograph to identify any interior or defective alloys. On the workshop floor, the process of die-casting is shown with metal being driven under pressure into the steel dies or molds. 04.30 Pacific Air Base, RNZAF Ground Crew: At an advance airbase in the Pacific, planes from the RNZAF are in for overhaul and maintenance. Once everything is thoroughly tested and repaired, the planes are off for more active service against the Japanese to "sweep the road to Tokyo."00:06:34Short FilmEngineering, History, New Zealand, ScienceG2010-01-2000:00:00
