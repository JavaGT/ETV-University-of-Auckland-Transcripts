pts/library_upload/5974_295616/Weekly_Review_No_246LIBRARY - Archive NZ - Weekly Review No 246Weekly Review No 246 (1946) Dunedin, New Massage School: A new school of physiotherapy is opened in Dunedin by prime minister Peter Fraser. Remedial exercise and massage is carried out by students on each other. 01.20 Sport, The Season Opens: Wellington schoolgirls meet for the basketball (netball) season's opening day tournament. Meanwhile at Eden Park in Auckland, Grammar take on Marist in the opening match of the new rugby season. 03.05 Health, Hospitals Meet Staff Problems: A tour of the new hospital in the Hutt Valley looking at all the hardworking people who make the facility run smoothly. The hospital kitchen has the latest mod-cons and large-scale cooking technology. The staff have their own dining room and accommodation. 06.05. At the port of Auckland, the American ship Flying Dragon is loading meat and making history - the first US ship to load a frozen cargo in New Zealand. The meat is bound for Britain where food rationing is still in place.
