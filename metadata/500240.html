etv/25670_tongariro_power_640x360LIBRARY - Archive NZ - Tongariro Power - 1970From a thousand-square-mile watershed, the latent force of many rivers is being channelled to the Tongariro Power Scheme - a gigantic hydro-electric development. Turangi township was built as the centre of the Tongariro Power Development. The scheme was developed to divert the water that flows from Ruapehu, Ngaruahoe, Tongariro from the Kaimanawa Ranges. The watershed area serving the scheme is more than 1000 square-miles, tunnelling covers 27 miles, canals 14 miles, four small dams and 1 large 200 foot concrete dam. Workers involved in the scheme total over 1500 scattered over a large areas. 03.00 good sequences of heavy machinery and SFX “As New Zealand’s cities and manufacturing industries grow, so does the need for power and the challenge of harnessing increasingly difficult sources of supply.”06.30 onwards – great pix of penstocks being installed 07.50 “To win power from the Volcanic Plateau means combatting the very forces that formed it.” 12.40 good sequence on Italian tunnellers here 15.00 “The power of rivers from more than 1000 square miles of country will be harnessed and led, for most of the way, underground in one of New Zealand’s most imaginative engineering undertakings.” Duration 16.1400:16:14G2014-05-2311:22:34
