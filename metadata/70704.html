On Friday, Campbell Live was with cabbie Sunny Singh as he went about his job at the Auckland International Airport taxi rank. Sunny is one of hundreds of drivers who are simply trying to provide for their families. Some people emailed in and said Sonny should give up being a cabbie. He'd love to, but he's tried to get other work and he can't. We're pretty sure the people who emailed us don't want Sunny to go on the dole. So, he drives a cab - or, he did. From tonight, the company he works for, President Taxis, no longer has a contract with the international airport. Business is so tight for smaller companies at the airport that some cabbies are protesting to the extent of going on hunger strike. It's both extremely serious and terribly sad that things have reached that stage. Sunny is upset because the cost of working out of the airport is so great that drivers with the smaller companies are struggling to make a living out of it. But the airport's a business, not a philanthropic trust. Is that their concern? Campbell Live met with Richard Barker, the airport's general manager, commercial and retail, to discuss the cabbie's lot. Feeding kids through school gardens. The garden-to-table programme is a return to old fashioned gardening lessons, but it's far from the days of boiled cabbage.
