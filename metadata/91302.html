etv/afrique_le_defi_vert_tv5_monde_french_20141009_1655_640x360Afrique le defi vertAdrar, the end of the oases? The Sahara desert covers most of the land in Mauritania. It scarcely ever rains there and water exists only in underground aquifers. For hundreds of years the nomadic populations have used oases to quench their thirst and water their herds. But the great drought of the eighties has changed all this. Faced with the urgency of the situation, local and international aid programmes have been set up: men and women are attempting to bring in new techniques, change behaviours, provide information and persuade people of the scale of the crisis. However, it often proves difficult to talk about sustainable development to people with no resources...
