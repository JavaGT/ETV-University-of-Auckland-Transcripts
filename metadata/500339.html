RAILWAYS Films, old photographs and modern events illustrate the inter-development of the railway, and New Zealand, over the past 100 years. Stills of Ferrymead line, GVs statue of Moorhouse. Nice sequence of building the Moorhouse tunnel. 03.40 good sequence starting in 1880’s talking about new lines and land opening up with new settlers. Good A-Class pix and then Ks and Js - rolled into diesel and electrification – and control room “Always on the move, the railways moved with the times.” 0.5.25 look to be a shot of train going over Matatoke Viaduct. Good railcar shots. Wellington station and passengers. An electronic computer goes to work on freight movements. Wool and lamb, grain and coal pix. Railways develops industry – their story in interwoven. Trains going onto Cook St ferry Aramoana – what she’s doing is as revolutionary as that first tunnel to Lyttelton (and a shot of steam train coming out of Moorhouse tunnel). “Its influence will extend far into the future.” Duration: 08.3800:08:38G2014-05-2610:03:10
