etv/-f2FKmMneXY_640x360ONLINE COLLECTIONS - HEALTH - PacemakerA Pacemaker is a miniaturized computer that is used to treat a slow heart beat. It is about the size of a couple of stacked silver dollars and weighs approximately 17-25 grams. It is usually surgically placed or implanted just under the skin in the chest area. The device sends a tiny electrical pulse down a thin coded wire, called a lead, into your heart. This stimulates the heart to beat. These impulses are very tiny and most people do not feel them.
