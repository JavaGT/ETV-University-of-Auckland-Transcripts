pts/library_upload/3695_293848/Weekly_Review_No_98LIBRARY - Archive NZ - Weekly Review No 98(1943) New Zealand Here and There: Wartime New Zealand has wartime jobs for everyone. At the Blind Institute in Auckland, men are working on an order for the navy. An army field ambulance squad trains in emergency recovery work, hauling an injured man up a sheer face and up a steep hillside. 02.45 Last Days of Tunisia: The British 1st Army gathers strength on the Tunisian border to drive the Axis forces right out of Africa. Enemy planes attack the supply ships but the accompanying destroyers fight back. South of Tunisia, the 8th Army receives badly needed supplies of ammunition. Rearmed, the soldiers move forward again and the RAF gives valuable air support. Dramatically, Tunisia falls and the people turn out in their thousands to welcome their liberators. The Allies have 200,000 prisoners of war to look after in huge camps. Yesterday Africa, tomorrow Europe.00:08:06Short FilmHistory, New ZealandG2010-01-2000:00:00
