pts/library_upload/3781_456156/Weekly_Review_No_152LIBRARY - Archive NZ - Weekly Review No 152(1944) NZ Squadrons Strike from Bougainville: From a base on the Solomon Islands, the NZ and American flyers strike at Japanese targets in the jungle hinterland and southern coast. A group of NZ Torpedo bomber crews receive a final briefing from an intelligence officer while ground crews arm the planes. The RNZAF bombers take off on their mission to attack Japanese airbases either side of the Buka Passage. These bases provided security for the main Japanese base at Rabaul, New Britain and supported their forces operating at other locations in the Solomon Islands. All the Allied planes return safely and the pilots and crews debrief while the ground crews rearm and refuel the bombers ready for their next run.00:09:24Short FilmHistory, New ZealandG2010-01-2000:00:00
