pts/library_upload/3826_678050/Weekly_Review_No_89LIBRARY - Archive NZ - Weekly Review No 89(1943) Aid for Orchardists: In the Nelson region there is a shortage of labour to pick and grade fruit in the area's orchards so the army comes to the rescue. Few of the soldiers have worked in orchards before but learn the new skills quickly. 02.05 Eating for a Living: Laboratory experiments on rats are being used to test fresh meat against dried meat being exported to Britain. The tests aim to determine that 2 tonnes of dried meat has the same food value as the 5 tonnes of fresh meat from which it was made. 03.30 Fiji Defence Force: Fiji is a British responsibility but the country also has its own defence force, part of the South Pacific Command.
