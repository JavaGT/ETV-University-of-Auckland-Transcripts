Upcoming elections in Zimbabwe On July 30 Zimbabweans will head to the polls for an election in which youth and social media could be determining factors. Half of the population is now under 25, and they will have unprecedented power in the first election since a coup toppled long-time leader Robert Mugabe. Of 23 candidates, current President Emmerson Mnangagwa’s main rival is Nelson Chamisa, 40, from the Movement for Democratic Change Alliance. We’ll explore the power of youth, worries about vote rigging, and the potential for change in a post-Mugabe era. Reunited families? One week after US President Donald Trump signed an Executive Order stopping the separation of families due to his administration’s ‘zero-tolerance’ immigration policy we’ll revisit stories from the detention centres as efforts to reunite more than 2,000 children with their parents are underway. It’s been a week of chaos as the various agencies coordinating immigrant enforcement and detention try to get families back together. This week Trump called for immediate deportation without due process, and there has been confusion about choices parents have in seeking asylum or getting deported.
