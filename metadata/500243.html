pts/library_upload/4260_477321/Weekly_Review_No_154LIBRARY - Archive NZ - Weekly Review No 154(1944) Rugby, Otago - Southland Game at Invercargill: A record wartime crowd at Rugby Park in Invercargill for the provincial match of the season. After a tight game, Otago run out the winners 11-9. 02.35 The Americans Depart, Major General Leonard F Wing Says Goodbye: The commander of the US 43rd Division thanks New Zealand for the rest and relaxation his troops enjoyed after their tour of duty in the Solomon Islands. He praises the medical facilities, the housing provided for servicemen, the beautiful countryside and the opportunities his men had to exercise on various types of terrain. He mentions the parades, ceremonies and decoration days his men have been involved in. He says one of the most pleasant memories has been the chance to play a game of American Football. 05.55 Italy, New Zealanders Advance on Rome: North of Cassino, Kiwi troops break through the German lines and have the enemy on the run. Along Route 5, village after village falls in NZ hands. Engineers sweep for mines along the roadside and the signal corp re-establishes telegraph communications. The country is so difficult that some units have to be supplied by mule trains. The town of Atina bears evidence of the fury of battle and the townspeople are struggling to survive after the retreating Germans plundered their food supplies. At a field dressing station, German prisoners receive medical aid. The Field Security Service questions refugees and receives intelligence that cleans up a pocket of remaining German resistance with the help of 25lb artillery guns.
