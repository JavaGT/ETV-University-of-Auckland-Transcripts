pts/library_upload/2255_876956/cold_virus_moleculeLIBRARY - E=MC2 - Looking into the Cold Virus MoleculeOne of the uses of Nuclear Magnetic Resonance, or NMR is to study protein and RNA structures and interactions. We are using Massey's new 3 million dollar 700 MHz NMR spectrometer to determine the structure of an RNA molecule from a cold virus, as well as to study how this molecule interacts with a viral protein. This interaction is required for replication of viruses, thus the results of this study might eventually be used to develop drugs to block viral replication.
