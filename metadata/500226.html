pts/library_upload/4046_180638/Weekly_Review_No_317LIBRARY - Archive NZ - Weekly Review No 317(1947) Seddon, Motorised Laundress: Mrs. Watson runs a traveling washing machine service in the town of Seddon in Marlborough. She drives a motorbike towing a covered wagon carrying a washing machine to private homes around the town. 01.00 Dunedin, Underwater Electric Supply: From Port Chalmers to Portabello, an electric cable is being laid along the seafloor of Otago Harbour to help supply the Otago Peninsula. This is the first time in New Zealand that a municipal underwater electric cable has been installed. 02.35 Radar Research, The Canterbury Project: Radar was one of the most important tools in World War Two and, in New Zealand, was extensively used to guard the country?s coastline. Graphics are used to explain how radar works and why ideal radar conditions are found when the northwest wind blows across on the Canterbury Plains. As part of the Canterbury Project, New Zealand and British scientists are studying the impact of weather on radar and send up instruments attached to a kite.
