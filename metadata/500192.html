pts/library_upload/3728_938411/Weekly_Review_No_389LIBRARY - Archive NZ - Weekly Review No 389(1949) Goodbye Pamir: Pamir was one of the famous Flying P-Liner sailing ships of the German shipping company F. Laeisz. She was the last commercial sailing ship to round Cape Horn, in 1949. This item shows Pamir leaving Wellington Harbour at the beginning of her journey back to Europe. 01.25 Maori Cave Paintings: The limestone country of North Otago was a favourite moa hunting area for the Maori. Caves in the rocky outcrops gave the hunting parties shelter and on some walls are paintings in red pigment showing all kinds of animals including birds and fish. One painting shows a whaling ship. 03.00 Fish Story, Trout and Eels: At Takaka at the top of the South Island, a local lady shows her collection of pet eels. She has been feeding some of these eels for over 20 years and they're so tame she is able to pull them out of the water. In North Taranaki, a local farmer has tamed eels, trout and ducks and at feeding time they are very playful. 04.45 Cricket, NZ Team for England: Top NZ cricketers train at Lancaster Park in Christchurch ahead of the final trial for the tour to England later in the year. Walter Hadlee, Bert Sutcliffe and John Reid are shown practicing and playing.00:08:30Short FilmHistory, New ZealandG2010-01-2000:00:00
