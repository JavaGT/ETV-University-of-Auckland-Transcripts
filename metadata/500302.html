etv/25615_new_zealand_marches__640x360LIBRARY - Archive NZ - New Zealand Marches On (1938) - AAOJ W5034/1399A film about civil engineering before the Second World War. The film looks at road and rail projects on both the North and South Island of New Zealand. 2K scan from 16mm combined print. 16th June 1938. Scenes of Ministry of Works and Development construction sites: Mohaka viaduct, opened by Minister of Public Works, Mr Bob Semple; 4ZB wireless mast, Dunedin; Fawkesworth Cutting; Hutt reclamation, Wellington; Karamia Harbour; Nelson aerodrome; Rangitata Irrigation Scheme, man made stream 40 miles long. "A record of New Zealand's increased mechanisation during post depression years. The transition from the pick and shovel to the bulldozer. Road and railway building by horses and cart and pick, shovel and wheelbarrow; construction of Mohaka railway viaduct, known as Bob Semple's meccano set; opening ceremony performed by Bob Semple - N.Z. Public Works Department on projects such as the Hutt River reclamation, retaining walls at Karamea Harbour, railway track construction on the South Island main trunk, Timaru Airport. Rangitata irrigation scheme and Waitaki River road bridge." - (from NFL Notes)00:19:07G2014-05-2010:29:09
