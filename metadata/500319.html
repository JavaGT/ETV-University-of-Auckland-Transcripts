In Rotorua the old folks' service centre is providing occupation and companionship for the elderly and the handicapped. Three days a week they come together and carry out work for local firms - anything from addressing envelopes to making letterboxes. This scheme, based on an English one, helps the elderly and the not-so-well to feel that they have a place and a useful job in society. RAILWAYS AND PLAYWAYS. For fifty years Christchurch has been wanting a new railway station, and now the foundation stone is laid. In Hamilton a large and new play-area is opened. BUILDING A PORT. The new port at Bluff is expected to become New Zealand's main meat and wool port, and the first major works are being carried out by a French firm. 08.20 CLUTHA FLOOD. After torrential rains the Clutha River bursts its banks in the worst flood since 19l9.
