pts/library_upload/123_902112/chasing_currentsLIBRARY - E=MC2 - Chasing CurrentsThe movement of waters around New Zealand is significant, with huge ocean currents streaming around our islands from the west and strong tides generating a regular maelstrom in Cook Strait. The basic physics defining this water motion also acts on somewhat smaller scales, causing eddies along coastlines and near harbours. <br>The same physics is controlling behaviour at still smaller scales like ocean waves. Finally, water motion plays a part in controlling microscopic life at the millimetre scale. NIWA uses sophisticated methods to study how ocean waters move and mix. The tools they use include vessels (from ocean-going ships to single-engine zodiacs), sampling devices that stay at sea for years at a time, drifting robots sending information to satellites, and powerful computer programs. <br> Featuring Craig Stevens, Marine Physics, National Institute of Water & Atmospheric Research (NIWA)00:05:43DocumentaryBiology, Natural History, ScienceG2006-12-1400:00:00
