etv/in_the_shadow_of_the_soho_20190912_2030_640x360In the Shadow of the Towers: Stuyvesant High on 9/11When the Twin Towers were attacked on September 11, 2001, students had just started their day at Stuyvesant, the prestigious New York City specialized public high school, blocks from the World Trade Center. Directed and produced by seven-time Emmy-winner Amy Schatz, In the Shadow of the Towers: Stuyvesant High on 9/11 offers a unique eyewitness perspective on that tragic day and its aftermath from former Stuyvesant students. The film revisits the events of 9/11 through conversations with eight Stuyvesant alumni who, as teenagers, lived through the attack and whose lives were forever changed by it. “People don’t really talk about the fact that there were kids there,” says Himanshu Suri. The former students recall a normal, sunny day, one of the first days of the school year, suddenly interrupted by disaster. “Absolutely everything changed that day,” recounts Ilya Feldsherov, who was 17 at the time. The film’s subjects describe the chaos of the day – witnessing the planes crash, fleeing from the encroaching dust cloud from the towers’ collapse, and their harrowing journeys home, often miles away in the city’s outer boroughs, including Queens, Brooklyn, and the Bronx.
