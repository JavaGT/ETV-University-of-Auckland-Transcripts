Nat ran the first web server in New Zealand, and worked for a decade in the United States where he co-wrote the bestselling Perl Cookbook. For over a decade, Nat chaired the O'Reilly Open Source Convention and the O'Reilly Where 2.0 Conference, and co-chaired the O'Reilly European Open Source Convention, the O'Reilly Emerging Telephony Conference, and the O'Reilly Bioinformatics Conference. Nat is creator of the Odious business plan, board member of The Perl Foundation, former editor for O'Reilly and Associates, and former project manager for Perl 6. He is a consultant with a wide range of activities. Recently returned to New Zealand from the USA, Nat is looking to build networks of open source innovators, entrepreneurs, and emerging technologists in New Zealand. He created and runs the annual Kiwi Foo Camp, a gathering of a variety of people from science, business, technology, arts, media and politics, where cross-discipline conversations and collaborations are fostered.
