pts/library_upload/3860_186827/Weekly_Review_No_166LIBRARY - Archive NZ - Weekly Review No 166(1944) Relief Food Ship for Greece: Crates of relief supplies are loaded onto a ship bound for Greece to help the people with whom so many New Zealand soldiers fought and died. 02.00 Open-Cast Coal, Parliamentary Team Visits Waikato: A parliamentary teams tours the model mining village of Bennydale on the Waikato coalfield where new state houses have been recently been built. The team visits an open-cast mine to view excavation work and talk to miners about the old dangerous and inefficient method of underground coal mining. Part of a local lake is also being drained to exposure a thick seam of coal on the lake bed. 06.35 Wellington's Eagle Club: Young Wellington women who are the wives and fiances of American servicemen have formed their own club. The women learn about the United States from guest speakers who discuss the nation's history, people and culture.
