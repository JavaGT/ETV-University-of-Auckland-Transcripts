pts/library_upload/3801_647737/Weekly_Review_No_150LIBRARY - Archive NZ - Weekly Review No 150(1944) Christ's College Boys Handle Modern War Equipment: Members of the Christ's College Cadet Corp work on their 25lb artillery gun, mortars and machine guns. The boys receive basic rifle training and use radio equipment as part of the signals unit. 01.55 Re-establishment Training Centre, Men With New Limbs Learn New Trades: At a new centre in Wellington, returned servicemen who have lost limbs in the war train in new jobs including making artificial legs. In a special mirror room, men can watch how they walk with new legs. One man talks about his new job making paua shell jewelry. A willow farm in the Hutt Valley provides the raw materials for basket weaving. The men also do leatherwork and cabinet making. The director of the centre says the disabled men don't want charity, they want real jobs. 05.45 Italian Front, Prime Minister Peter Fraser Visits Cassino Battlefield: The PM is greeting by General Bernard Fryberg at the beginning of his visit to the New Zealand Division to see how the Kiwi soldiers are faring after 6 months intense fighting in Italy. Fraser's party travels to Cassino that fell to the Allies just a week before along the road are grim reminders of the recent fighting. The PM climbs Monastery Hill where the most desperate battles of the whole Mediterranean campaign were fought.00:09:26Short FilmHistory, New ZealandG2010-01-2000:00:00
