pts/library_upload/121_489349/radio_carbon_datingLIBRARY - E=MC2 - How Old is that Mummy? Radio Carbon DatingThe Rafter Radiocarbon Laboratory has a long history in radiocarbon dating. To determine the radiocarbon age of an organic material it is necessary to measure the proportion of radiocarbon (C14) in the carbon that it contains. The technical problem to be solved is the detection of the rare isotope C14 in the presence of the much more abundant isotopes C12 and C13. The natural abundance of 14C is about one 14C atom per trillion (1012) atoms of C12. <br> What can you date? Anything containing carbon between 150 and 60,000 years old (i.e.: wood, leather, bone, paper, seawater, gases, ice cores, pollen, pottery, coral, seeds, charcoal, blood residues, sediment, soil, shell, textiles, plant and animal tissue, insect remains, cave paintings, resins and glues). <br> Featuring Christine Prior and Rodger Sparks - radiocarbon scientists.00:07:00Short FilmArchaeology, ScienceG2006-12-1400:00:00
