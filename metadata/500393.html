pts/library_upload/5881_947910/TheDNAdealerLIBRARY - HOTSCIENCE - The DNA DealerThe DNA Dealer: Researchers at Lincoln University in Canterbury are looking into sheep genetics in order to help farmers improve the quality of their sheep. Currently, lamb mortality within the first few days of birth results in major economic losses to the sheep industry. It also poses a major welfare concern, with piles of dead lambs at farm gates providing an unsightly reminder of the "cost" of lambing in paddocks. While some lamb deaths can be attributed to specific causes such as birthing difficulties (dystocia), poor mothering, lamb misadventure and disease, approximately a third of them are associated with cold exposure and starvation.
